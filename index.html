ADHD planning doesn't need to be perfect ‚Äî just consistent.

1. Start with 3 wins, not 30 tasks +

Pick only 3 important tasks for the day. When you finish them, the day is already a success. Any extra tasks are bonuses, not proof of your worth.

2. Make your planner visible, not hidden +

Leave your planner open on your desk, bed or next to your laptop. ADHD brains forget things when they are out of sight ‚Äî keep your page where your eyes land.

3. Use ‚Äúdopamine breaks‚Äù instead of guilt breaks +

After one focused block (10‚Äì25 minutes), allow a tiny reward: music, stretch, snack or a scroll break with a timer. The goal is to come back, not to be perfect.

4. Your planner is not a report card +

Empty boxes do not mean failure. They mean you get more information about what your energy actually looks like. Adjust, don't attack yourself.

5. For the distracted heart (ADHD) +

Your brain is not the enemy. It just needs shorter steps, clearer boxes and kinder words. Choose a planner layout that feels like support, not like a judge ‚Äî and build your year one tiny page at a time.
